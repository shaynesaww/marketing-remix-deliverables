A note from me

Before anything else, I want you to know I heard you.

Megha,

I've been thinking about our conversation since the moment we hung up, and I wanted to put something in your hands before we talk about proposals or numbers or any of that.

Because the truth is, what you're building deserves more than a quote. It deserves to be understood first. So that's what this is. This is me showing you that I was actually listening.

When we talked, you said that being visible is a scary thing for you, and I didn't breeze past that. The thing is, almost everything we're about to build will ask you to be more visible than you've ever been, and that means my whole job is to make that feel safe. You shouldn't have to carry the exposure on your own. You get to show up as yourself, and the rest of us handle the machinery around you so that you never feel exposed doing it.

The other thing that stuck with me was how you actually do business. You told me the story about Bollywood, how they came to you not because you knew entertainment law, but because you had taken care of someone's family and they trusted you, and they said we'll pay you to learn. That's the whole thing, right? You've built a twenty-five year practice on trust and care instead of marketing, and that's exactly why this is going to work. Because what we're really doing here is taking the way you already make people feel, and giving it a place to live online.

And then there's another reason underneath all of it. You grew up watching your father go through what he went through, and maybe, now you want to hand this next generation of founders the security and the belief that nobody ever handed you. That's much more of a mission than it is a course. And it changes everything about how I would build this.

So here is what I've laid out below. It isn't the proposal yet. Think of it as the shape of what's possible, the picture of where we could take this together, so that when we do sit down to talk specifics, we are already on the same page about how big and how meaningful this really is.

Read it slow. It's about you.

What you're building

A way in that doesn't ask anyone to leave home.

You're building a place where the smartest, most capable young people in India can learn how to bring their ideas into the U.S. market without moving across the world to do it.

No visa. No uprooting their life. And no having to choose between their dream and the security their families want for them. They stay right where they are, and you help them build something that reaches all the way over here.

And the beauty of it is that it works on three levels at once. The founder finally gets a path forward. Their parents get the reassurance that their child is not being pulled away from them. And the country keeps its talent at home, which is exactly what the Indian government is pushing for right now. So you are not fighting against the culture. Seems like a win for all.

You don't need a visa. Stay home.

They're the same journey, caught at two different moments. So instead of building two separate things, we build one path, and we meet each person wherever they happen to be standing on it.

The Dreamer, Still Employed

Talented, well educated, often working for a U.S. company already. They have the idea and the late nights, but they haven't taken the leap, because the culture they grew up in rewards safety and the cost of failing feels too high.

What they need from you: permission, encouragement, and the first real steps.
Further along

The Founder, Already Moving

They've already leapt. They've registered, they may have government seed funding, and they're making sales. Now they want to formally establish in the U.S. and grow, and they need a real guide and real resources to get there.

What they need from you: a closer relationship, and the people who can actually make it happen.

The brand

It has to feel unmistakably like you.

Your law firm site is clean and gold and white and authoritative, and it does its job beautifully. But it's only one side of you. The version of you I saw on Instagram is warmer than that, and brighter, and far more colorful, and that is the version this platform gets to be built around.

So we won't hand you a template with your name dropped into it. We'll build the brand out of who you actually are. Your photos, your stories, the way you talk, the colors you already lean toward, the culture you come from. It needs to be authoritative enough that a serious founder trusts it, and warm enough that they fall in love with it. Clean and credible on one hand, colorful and alive on the other. That balance is the hard part, and it's exactly the part worth getting right.

I want to be careful with my words here, because this matters more to me than anything else on this page.

And honestly, that fits how you've always worked anyway. You have never kept a client for a single transaction in your life.
