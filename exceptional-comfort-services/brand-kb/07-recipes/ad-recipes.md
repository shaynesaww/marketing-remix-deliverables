# ECS Ad Recipes

How to write ad copy for ECS without slipping into the high-pressure, fear-based default of HVAC advertising. The point of every ECS ad is to sound like a real person, not a panic salesman.

**Build order for any ad:**
1. Pick the recipe below.
2. Pull the atoms it references.
3. Draft 3 variants (headline + body + CTA).
4. Run anti-AI strip (zero em dashes, no fragments, vary "no obligation" phrasing, no "no upsell" promises).
5. Hand off for image generation (Steven's Higgsfield workflow or Marketing Remix).

---

## Universal ad rules

- **No fear language.** Banned phrases: "your AC is failing," "before it breaks down," "act fast," "limited time," "don't wait."
- **No false urgency.** ECS doesn't run "TODAY ONLY" anything.
- **Steven's voice translates differently to ads than to social.** Tighter. No "look" or "you know." Still warm, still real.
- **Hook → Promise → Proof → Ask.** Every ad fits this four-beat structure.
- **Local always.** Mention the Upstate or a specific town in every ad.
- **One claim per ad.** Don't try to be everything at once.
- **No "no upsell" phrasing.** Show through stories or proof, never promise.

# Recipe 1 — Cold Traffic, "Honest HVAC" Angle

**Goal:** Introduce ECS to homeowners who don't know us. Lean on integrity and honest diagnosis.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `04-positioning/why-ecs-vs-big-shops.md`
- `00-core/identity.md` (three pillars)
- One proof story (`03-customer-stories/filter-not-compressor.md` or `03-customer-stories/eighty-six-year-old-chandelier.md`)

**Format:**

**Headline (3 variants):**
- "HVAC in the Upstate, done the way it should be."
- "Family-run HVAC. We answer the phone."
- "Honest diagnosis. Fair pricing. No high-pressure sales."

**Body:**
```
Steven and Angel Tibball run ECS out of the Upstate. Twelve years in the trade. Licensed, bonded, insured.

If your AC, furnace, or heat pump needs a look, we'll come out, tell you what's actually wrong, and quote you fairly. No fear tactics. No upsell theater.

Serving Pickens, Easley, Greer, Seneca, Greenville, and Lake Keowee.
```

**CTA:** "Get a real diagnosis" → free estimate form
**Image direction:** Steven in front of his truck, or Steven + Angel together. Clean, professional, NOT cheesy stock-photo.

# Recipe 6 — "Got Overcharged Before?" Recovery Ad

**Goal:** Reach homeowners burned by a big shop in the past. Most polarizing of the ad set — deploy carefully.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `03-customer-stories/janice-overcharged-coil.md`
- `04-positioning/pricing-philosophy.md`

**Format:**

**Headline:** "Got a quote that didn't feel right? Get a second opinion."

**Body:**
```
A neighbor recently paid a regional HVAC company thousands of dollars to replace a coil. The coil was fine. The unit just had too much refrigerant in it.

If a quote from somebody else feels off, we'll come out for a real diagnosis. Sometimes you need the repair. Sometimes you don't. Either way, we'll tell you straight.
```

**CTA:** "Get a second opinion"
**Image direction:** Clean, professional. Avoid villainizing imagery.
**Cap:** This ad uses the industry-critique angle. Per guardrails, limit to one critique-angle ad in rotation at any time.

# Local Service Ads (LSAs)

LSAs are Google's pay-per-lead format with the green checkmark. Different rules.

**For LSAs:**
- Headline is fixed by Google (you can't customize).
- Business description (about us) is the customizable copy.
- Pull from `00-core/identity.md` (motto + three pillars).
- Stay within character limits.
- The "Google Guaranteed" trust signal comes from the verification process, not the copy.

**LSA description template:**
```
Family-run HVAC in the Upstate. Steven and Angel Tibball, licensed, bonded, and insured. Honest diagnosis, fair pricing, no high-pressure sales. AC repair and install, heating, maintenance plans, generator service, Lake Keowee specialty equipment. We answer the phone.
```

# Image direction (across all ads)

Steven runs his own image generation via Higgsfield. The brand visual rules:

- Clean and professional. No cartoon characters. No cheesy stock-photo families.
- Real-feeling. A truck on a real driveway beats a fake AI vista.
- Branded. ECS logo present and rendered correctly (occasionally AI mangles it — Steven re-runs when that happens).
- Upstate-feeling when geographic. Wooded, residential, real.
- No fear imagery. No "broken-down system" panic visuals. No "drowning in heat" tropes.
