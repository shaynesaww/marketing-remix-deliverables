# ECS Email Recipes

How to write any ECS email — sequences, one-offs, follow-ups, newsletters — using the brand KB atoms.

**Build order for any email:**
1. Pick the recipe below.
2. Pull the atoms it references.
3. Write a 2-paragraph draft (most ECS emails should be short).
4. Run voice test and anti-AI strip.
5. Sign as Steven (or Angel for office emails).

---

## Universal email rules

- **Short.** Most ECS emails should be under 200 words. Steven and Angel are not corporate marketers and shouldn't sound like them.
- **From a real person.** Sign with the actual name. Not "The ECS Team."
- **Plain text feel.** Even if HTML, keep formatting minimal. No big graphics, no fancy buttons. Maybe one CTA link.
- **Subject lines:** under 60 characters, conversational. Not "Limited Time AC Tune-Up Offer!!! 🔥"
- **Anti-AI rules apply.** Zero em dashes. No fragments. No "we're committed to."
- **No fear-based urgency.** "Schedule before your AC fails!" is out. "Your maintenance is due this month — want to set something up?" is in.

# Recipe 1 — New Lead Follow-Up (after they filled out a form)

**Goal:** Confirm the lead, set expectation on when Steven will call, build warmth.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `00-core/identity.md` (the three pillars — one sentence)
- `04-positioning/why-ecs-vs-big-shops.md` (the "I answer the phone" beat — one line)

**Template:**

```
Subject: Got your message — Steven will call you back today

Hey [first name],

Just got your message about [service]. I'll give you a call back today before [time] to talk through what's going on.

If you need to reach me sooner, my direct line is [number]. I'll pick up if I can — and if I'm driving, I'll pull over and call right back.

Talk soon,
Steven Tibball
Exceptional Comfort Services
```

**Notes:** Keep it under 60 words. The point is to confirm receipt and set the expectation that a real person is responding, not an autoresponder.

# Recipe 2 — Post-Service Thank You

**Goal:** Close the loop, set up a review request, leave the door open for the next call.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `00-core/identity.md` (warm-and-fuzzy ethos)
- `03-customer-stories/pipe-insulation-no-charge.md` (the small-things-matter framing — adapt loosely)

**Template:**

```
Subject: Thanks for letting us in today

Hey [first name],

Just wanted to say thank you for trusting us with your [service]. It was a pleasure being in your home today.

If you ever notice anything off with the system or have questions about what we did, give me a call. No charge for the conversation.

If you'd take a minute and leave us a Google review, it'd mean a lot. Here's the link: [GBP review link]

Thanks again,
Steven
```

**Notes:** Send within 24 hours of the job. Review request after the relationship is established, not before.

# Recipe 3 — Quote Follow-Up (lead got a quote, hasn't decided)

**Goal:** Stay top of mind without pressure.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `04-positioning/pricing-philosophy.md` (fair pricing stance)
- `04-positioning/why-ecs-vs-big-shops.md`

**Template:**

```
Subject: Following up on your [service] quote

Hey [first name],

Wanted to follow up on the quote I sent you last week for [service]. No pressure — just making sure it didn't get lost in your inbox.

If you have questions, want to walk through anything, or want me to come back out and look at something I missed, just let me know.

If you've decided to go a different direction, that's fine too. Either way, I appreciate you considering us.

Steven
```

**Notes:** Send 5-7 days after the quote. Never follow up more than twice. After two follow-ups, leave them alone unless they reach out.

# Recipe 4 — Maintenance Plan Reminder

**Goal:** Remind a maintenance customer their visit is due, make it easy to schedule.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `02-services/maintenance-plans.md` (`needs-info` — Steven still owes plan details, but the spirit of the service is there)
- `03-customer-stories/pipe-insulation-no-charge.md` (the kind of work we do on maintenance)

**Template:**

```
Subject: Time for your spring [or fall] maintenance check

Hey [first name],

Spring [or fall] is here, which means it's time for your scheduled maintenance visit. We'll get the system tuned up and ready for the season.

A few options for scheduling:
- Reply to this email with a couple of days that work
- Call/text me at [number]
- Book online: [link]

If anything's been off with the system since we were out last, mention it when you book and I'll plan a little extra time.

Steven
```

# Recipe 5 — Seasonal "Get Ahead of It" Email

**Goal:** Light educational nudge before peak season. Not aggressive selling.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `03-customer-stories/filter-not-compressor.md` (filter awareness)
- Any educational social post (3.1-3.7) for content seed

**Template:**

```
Subject: One thing to check before AC season

Hey [first name],

Quick note from the team — peak AC season is about a month out, and the most common reason a system fails in the first heat wave is a dirty filter.

Five minutes of your time. Change it if it's been more than 3 months.

If you do that and your system still acts up, give us a call. We do honest diagnostics — no scare tactics, no "your unit is failing" speeches.

Steven
```

**Notes:** Once per season, maximum. Never use scare-language subject lines like "Your AC is going to fail."

# Recipe 6 — "Steven moment" Story Email (Newsletter-Style)

**Goal:** Build relationship over time. One real story per month from the field.

**Atoms to pull:**
- Pick any `03-customer-stories/*.md` not used recently
- `steven-voice-profile.md` voice rules apply at full strength

**Template:**

```
Subject: [Something true and intriguing — "An 86-year-old, a chandelier, and an electrician who showed up before I left"]

Hey [first name],

A story from this week.

[2-3 paragraphs telling the story in third-person narrative voice, with one or two of Steven's direct quotes]

That's how we operate around here.

Steven
```

**Notes:** Monthly newsletter cadence. Always a real story. Never a "sale."

# Recipe 7 — Holiday / Year-End Note

**Goal:** Warmth, gratitude, no selling.

**Atoms to pull:**
- `01-story/angel-the-partner.md`
- `00-core/identity.md`

**Template:**

```
Subject: Thanks for an incredible year

Hey [first name],

Angel and I just wanted to send a quick note before the year wraps up.

Whatever the size of the job you trusted us with this year — emergency call, install, maintenance, the occasional chandelier referral — we don't take any of it for granted. ECS is a year and a half old, and the only reason we're still standing is the customers who picked us when they could've called anyone.

Have a good holiday season. We'll see you in the new year.

Steven & Angel
```

# What this file is NOT

- Not a place to write marketing-sounding "drip sequences."
- Not where transactional emails (booking confirmations, etc.) get drafted — those live in the GoHighLevel CRM workflow.
- Not a high-volume newsletter pipeline. ECS is a small business; emails are an extension of relationships, not a broadcast channel.
