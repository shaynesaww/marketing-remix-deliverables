## The kids

Steven and Angel have three kids, all born in Greenville. The oldest is 22.

Two of them are already involved with ECS in some form:

- **Aiden** (older son) — works part-time for ECS as a tech. Steven is in conversation with him about moving him to full-time once the marketing is dialed in and there's enough work to support the hire.
- **Amelia** (daughter) — helps with social media, especially TikTok content.

Both will be represented on the website. Both their names are approved by Steven for use on ECS surfaces.

## The standards Steven holds his son to

Aiden has talked about being a manager someday. Steven is working on humility with him. From the call:

> "I'm like, he's like 'man, I could be a manager.' I was like, you're 22 years old. You still call me at least twice a week because you're having problems. When you can make it two, three weeks without having to call me for something, then maybe you're ready to take on more responsibilities."

Steven also told him directly he wouldn't pay him the formula that turns techs into ripoff artists — *"I'm not paying you $35 an hour plus an 8% commission. That's where business owners are failing."*

## The 10-year vision

Steven wants ECS run by the family — him and Angel, with quality people hired alongside them. He's going to stay hands-on:

> "I'm going to be heavily involved. It's not going to be one of these where I'm going to just disappear. I'll take my vacations and whatever, but I'm going to be part of all of it. We're not going to use subcontractors. This is going to be our company."

He's explicit about what he won't do: he won't sell to a private equity roll-up. He won't grow into something he doesn't recognize. He wants ECS to stay local, stay family, and not change who they are as they get bigger.
