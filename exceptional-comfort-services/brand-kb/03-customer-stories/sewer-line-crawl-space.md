## A river under the house

This was back in Steven's days at the big company. The call came in on a summer afternoon. A woman renting a house had no AC, and the heat was sitting heavy on her.

Steven pulled up and went around back to look at the condenser. Before he ever got to the unit, he noticed the grass. It was discolored in a long stripe running away from the house, the kind of dark patch you get when something has been saturating the ground for a long time. He looked closer and figured out what he was looking at. A sewer line in the crawl space had busted and nobody had caught it. It had been running for years.

He opened the crawl space hatch and looked under. It was, in his words, like a river under there. Most techs would have closed the hatch right there. The sewer situation is the perfect excuse to write up a return trip, leave, and never come back.

Steven went in anyway. The customer needed AC. He worked his way to the air handler, got his diagnostic going, and found the actual fault. The zone board had failed. He had one on the truck, swapped it out, and got her AC running the same day.

## What the story shows

Some techs use a bad crawl space as a way out. Steven sees it as somebody who still needs their AC. The job isn't fun and it isn't glamorous, but the homeowner doesn't pay extra for the fact that you climbed into the mess to fix it.
