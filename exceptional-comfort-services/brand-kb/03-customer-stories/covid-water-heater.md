## The sign on the front door

This was during COVID. Steven pulled up to the house and there was a sign on the front door that said, in plain letters, do not enter, COVID. The homeowner was inside, sick, and couldn't leave.

His water heater was leaking. The floor underneath it had been rotting for who knows how long. Steven didn't do water heaters at the time. That wasn't really part of what he was running calls for. The customer needed it handled though, and he was homebound, so calling around to a different company wasn't going to be easy on him.

Steven called his service manager and asked him to bring a water heater out. They got it on the truck and out to the house the same day. The customer stayed in the next room while Steven worked. Old one out, new one in, floor cleaned up. Done.

## What the story shows

The job wasn't in his wheelhouse and the customer was contagious. Steven figured it out anyway because the alternative was leaving a sick guy with a leaking water heater rotting out his floor. That's the bar for what counts as taking care of somebody.
