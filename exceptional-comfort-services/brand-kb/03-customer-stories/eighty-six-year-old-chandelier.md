## A call that wasn't actually about HVAC

Steven was at an 86-year-old customer's house on a regular service call. While he was there, she mentioned the chandelier in her dining room. The roofers had been on her house recently and something they did up top had knocked it loose. It was hanging crooked.

He told her straight that he couldn't fix it. Electrical work needs an electrician, and he isn't licensed for that side of it. Normally he'd email a homeowner a couple of names so she could call around herself. She doesn't use email.

So Steven sat down in her living room and started looking. He didn't want to send her to one of the big outfits that does both HVAC and electrical, because the second she opened the door for a chandelier they'd be quoting her a new system. He kept scrolling until he found an electrician who only did electrical. Called him on her behalf.

The guy showed up before Steven even finished his own paperwork and left.

## What the story shows

The job was done. Steven could have packed up and headed to the next call. He stayed because she was 86, she couldn't email, and the easy path led right to somebody who'd take advantage of her. That extra hour wasn't on any invoice. It's the reason she'll be a customer for the rest of her life.
