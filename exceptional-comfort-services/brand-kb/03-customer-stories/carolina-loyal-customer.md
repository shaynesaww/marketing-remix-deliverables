## "I don't want anybody else here but you"

Back at the big company Steven came up at, there was a customer who got attached. Every call, he wanted Steven. Steven kept getting put on the route.

Then Steven got promoted to service manager. He stopped going out to houses and started running 30 techs from the shop. The next time the customer's unit needed work, the company sent a different guy. The customer called the office, not happy.

> "I don't want anybody else here but you."

Steven told him he couldn't run the truck anymore, but he had an idea. His older son was coming up in the trade by then. He asked the customer how he'd feel about his son showing up next time. The customer agreed to try it. After that, he wouldn't let anybody but Steven's son on the property either.

A couple of years later, after Steven left to start ECS, he drove back over to talk to the guy in person and let him know where to find him. The customer had sold the house and moved.

The new owner answered the door. He's an ECS customer now.

## What the story shows

The relationship is the asset, not the company logo on the truck. When a customer follows a name across jobs, then his replacement keeps the relationship going, the business that gets built is the kind you can hand down. That's the whole point of ECS being a family company.
