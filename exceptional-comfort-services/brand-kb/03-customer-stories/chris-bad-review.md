## The customer who never forgot getting sold

Chris was a customer at the big company Steven came up at. A tech got sent out to his house, looked at his unit, and told him it was failing and needed to be replaced. Chris didn't buy it. He didn't agree to the system either.

What he did do was leave a one-star Google review on his way out the door. And then he kept that review alive. Every year for four years, he'd log back into Google and update the review so it would stay near the top of the listing. He wanted other homeowners to see it before they called.

That kind of grudge tells you something. He wasn't a difficult customer. He was a customer who'd been burned and refused to let it slide.

Recently he called Steven directly. He didn't want a sales pitch. He wanted an actual estimate from somebody who'd tell him the truth about whether his system needed to be replaced or not. He found Steven through a connection and went straight to the source.

## What the story shows

The customers who got sold something they didn't need don't forget. They wait. When they finally call somebody they trust, they're not looking for a deal. They're looking for an honest answer.
