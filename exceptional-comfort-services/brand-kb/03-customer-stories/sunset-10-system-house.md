## A house with ten systems and a wine cellar

The first maintenance agreement Steven ever sold under the ECS name was up in Sunset, in the Lake Keowee area. The house has ten HVAC systems running across it, plus a dedicated wine cellar with its own climate control. Fresh air intakes, energy recovery, dehumidifiers, the works. The kind of place where the equipment list takes a while to walk through.

The maintenance agreement itself was three thousand dollars. That was a real signal early on that the model could work. A premium home with serious equipment needs a service company that actually understands how all those pieces talk to each other, and the homeowner wanted somebody he could call and trust, not a different tech every time.

Since signing that first agreement, ECS has done roughly twenty thousand dollars worth of work for that customer. Repairs, upgrades, the kind of small jobs that keep a complicated system running. The relationship kept building because every visit ended the same way. Done right, no upsell, no drama.

## What the story shows

Lake Keowee homes don't run on the same equipment most houses do, and the customers who own them notice fast when a tech is in over his head. Steven took on that first big house with the right knowledge and the right approach, and the work kept coming because of it. The maintenance agreement isn't a one-time sale. It's the start of a long relationship with the house.
