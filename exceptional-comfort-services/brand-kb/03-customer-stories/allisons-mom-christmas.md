## The Christmas no-heat call

It was wintertime and Allison's mom had no heat. Allison is a friend of the family, and her mom didn't have a lot of money sitting around for a furnace repair right before Christmas.

Steven went out and pulled the cover off the air handler. Whoever had wired the unit originally hadn't tightened the connections down. The wire nuts had worked loose over time, and the wires inside were melted where the current had been arcing across the gap. It's a common thing when an install gets rushed. The fix is cheap if you know what you're looking at, and it takes about twenty minutes.

Steven decided right there he wasn't going to charge her for it.

> "She didn't really have a whole lot of money, so I told her not to worry about it. Just fixed it up for her and told her merry Christmas."

He asked her if she'd be willing to leave him a Google review, and she did.

## What the story shows

The fix was small, but the moment wasn't. A free repair around the holidays is the kind of thing a big shop on a sales quota would never let a tech do, and it's the kind of thing Steven still does on his own clock.
