## A tradesman who only refers people he'd trust in his own house

Sam is a plumbing tech at the same company where Angel works. He and Steven got to know each other through her, and over time Sam has quietly turned into one of the best things that ever happened to ECS.

Sam's own house uses Steven. His five rental properties use Steven. His dad uses Steven. His wife uses Steven. When a couple of his friends started building out a gym and needed mini-splits, they used Steven too.

Steven puts the running total of Sam's referrals somewhere in the thirty to fifty thousand dollar range so far, and it keeps growing.

The reason Sam refers that much work isn't loyalty for the sake of loyalty. Sam is a quality-focused plumber himself. He talks to homeowners respectfully, he does the work right, and he won't put his name behind somebody who doesn't operate the same way. He only refers people he'd be comfortable with in his own house.

The favors run both directions. Sam's backup camera quit working in his truck not long ago. Steven took a look and fixed it for him.

## What the story shows

The strongest endorsement in the trades comes from another tradesman who has nothing to gain. Sam isn't getting a kickback. He's just protecting his own reputation by sending people to somebody he trusts, and the work keeps stacking up because that trust got earned one job at a time.
