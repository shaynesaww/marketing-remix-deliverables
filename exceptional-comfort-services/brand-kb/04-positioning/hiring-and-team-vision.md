## The kind of tech ECS hires

Honest. Comfortable talking to people. High integrity. Shows up on time, in uniform, ready to walk into someone's house and represent the brand. Clean cut. Not pulling on his work shirt in the driveway five minutes before the appointment.

Steven calls it "the whole package." It's the technician who can hold a real conversation with an 80-year-old at her kitchen table, then go diagnose the unit correctly, and then explain in plain English what's wrong without making her feel talked down to.

## The kind of tech ECS won't hire

Cocky. Arrogant. Know-it-all. The car-salesman type who walks into a homeowner's house already calculating his commission. Steven has seen plenty of them. He's managed plenty of them.

The hard truth about that profile — they usually know enough about the trade to be convincing. But they bring habits picked up at the big shops, and those habits don't unlearn easy. The script for selling fear instead of fixing the unit. The reflex to quote a system when a part would do. The instinct to make the customer feel like she got a deal when she actually got a bill.

> "You've got to undo whatever nonsense they've got going on first."

A tech like that often costs more than he brings in, because the customers he closes are the customers who never call again.

## Humility is the prerequisite

Steven says it plainly.

> "You have got to be humbled in this career. I've been humbled a couple of times."

He's shocked himself. He's almost fallen out of attics. He's fallen off ladders. He's hung from attic hatches when the ceiling wouldn't hold him. That's the trade. Electrical mistakes start fires. Refrigerant mistakes burn skin. A misstep on a roof is a hospital trip.

A tech who hasn't been humbled yet is dangerous to himself and dangerous to the homeowner. Steven won't put a guy in a truck before he's ready, and he won't let his own son skip steps either. The older boy has talked about being a manager at 22. Steven's answer is that he'll get there, but not by jumping the line.

## The team vision

ECS stays family-run, with quality people hired alongside. No subcontractors. No franchise expansion. No selling to private equity in five years. Steven stays in the trucks, double-checking the work, training the new techs the right way, and keeping the standard tight enough that it can grow without going soft.
