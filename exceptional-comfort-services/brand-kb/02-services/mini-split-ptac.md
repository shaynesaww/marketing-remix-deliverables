## What ECS does

ECS installs, services, and repairs both mini-split systems and PTAC units across the Upstate. On the mini-split side that includes single-zone and multi-zone setups for additions, garages, sunrooms, gyms, and whole-house ductless conversions. On the PTAC side that includes the through-the-wall units common in hotels, condos, and some rental properties, with both service and replacement work.

## Who it's for

Homeowners adding conditioned space without the ductwork to support it. Garage gyms and workshop builds. Sunroom and addition retrofits. Condo and rental owners running PTAC units that need regular service or end-of-life replacement. Property owners who'd rather work with an HVAC tech who actually does both equipment types instead of one who only knows traditional split systems.

## How Steven approaches it

Mini-splits are getting more popular in residential builds across this market, and Steven has been installing them long enough to know which scenarios actually call for one and which scenarios are better served by a conventional system. He'll talk a homeowner through both options before quoting. A mini-split is the right answer sometimes and the wrong answer other times, and selling somebody the wrong system to hit a number isn't how ECS operates.

PTAC work is steadier and less glamorous. The units are simpler, but they fail more often because they get used hard. Steven services them the same way he services any other unit, which means actually opening the cabinet and looking at what's going on.

## What ECS won't do or claim

No pushing a mini-split into a home that would be better served by a regular split system. No replacing a PTAC when a service call can keep it running another season or two. No skipping the basic checks because the unit "looks fine."
