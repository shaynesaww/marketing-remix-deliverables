## What ECS does

ECS services and repairs whole-home and portable generators across the Upstate. ECS is a certified Generac dealer for service. When an existing generator reaches end of life and the gas line and electrical infrastructure are already in place, ECS can replace the unit in that existing footprint.

## Who it's for

Homeowners who already own a generator and need annual service, repairs, or eventual replacement. Lake Keowee second-home owners who can't afford to lose power. Customers who already had a generator installed by another company and want a more responsive service partner going forward.

## How Steven approaches it

Generator work is one of the areas where doing the job right and respecting the licensing rules matter at the same time. South Carolina requires a separate plumbing license and a separate electrical license to do a brand-new generator installation from scratch, because the work touches both the gas line and the home's main electrical panel. Steven holds the HVAC contractor's license. He does not hold those other two licenses, and he isn't going to pretend otherwise.

So the line is drawn cleanly. Service and repair on any generator, yes. Replacement of an existing generator in an existing footprint where the gas and electrical are already in place, yes. Brand-new installation from scratch is a different conversation and only happens in limited cases for existing ECS customers where Steven's brother-in-law (a master electrician) can be brought in and the customer pulls the permits themselves.

That's the honest version. The website language has to reflect it.

## What ECS won't do or claim

ECS does not advertise or perform new generator installation as a standard offering. ECS is not licensed in South Carolina for the plumbing or electrical scopes that a new install legally requires. Any claim or implication otherwise gets cut from copy on sight.
