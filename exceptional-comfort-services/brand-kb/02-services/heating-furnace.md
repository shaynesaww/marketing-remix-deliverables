## What ECS does

ECS services, repairs, and maintains gas furnaces across the Upstate. That covers seasonal tune-ups, no-heat calls in the middle of January, intermittent failures, ignition issues, flame-sensor problems, blower troubles, gas valve replacements, and the dozens of smaller failures that show up on a furnace once it's a few seasons old.

## Who it's for

Homeowners running a gas furnace as their primary heat. Folks in Pickens, Easley, Greenville, Seneca, and the surrounding area who want their furnace looked at by somebody who actually understands how the thing works. Customers who'd rather have the unit cleaned and tuned than replaced.

## How Steven approaches it

Steven came up in the trade running both AC and heat. Twelve years of service calls and four-plus years managing a 30-tech crew means he's worked through nearly every common failure on a residential furnace. He doesn't open the panel looking for a reason to sell something. He opens it looking for what's wrong.

When a furnace stops working in winter, the cause is usually small. A bad flame sensor. A loose wire nut. A pressure switch that's done. The fix is rarely a new furnace, and Steven will tell a homeowner that straight. He told a story on the onboarding call about a friend's mom around Christmas whose unit had melted wire nuts inside the air handler. The fix took twenty minutes. He didn't charge her.

## What ECS won't do or claim

No fear language about a furnace being unsafe when it isn't. No pitching a replacement on a unit that has years of life left. If there's a real safety concern like a cracked heat exchanger or a serious gas issue, Steven will explain it plainly and back it up with what he sees.
