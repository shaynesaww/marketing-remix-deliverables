## What ECS does

ECS serves the high-end second-home market around Lake Keowee, including Sunset and the surrounding lake communities. The work covers everything a primary home gets, plus the equipment that shows up more often in premium builds: energy recovery ventilators, fresh-air intakes, whole-house dehumidifiers, steam humidifiers, multi-system layouts, and geothermal systems on the service side. Lake Jocassee is outside the service area because the drive is too long to support good response times.

## Who it's for

Owners of lake homes around Keowee who want their equipment looked after by somebody they actually want in their house. People whose second homes need scheduled maintenance because they're not on-site to catch problems early. Property managers and caretakers handling multi-system homes where keeping track of every unit takes a real partner.

## How Steven approaches it

These homes have different equipment and different expectations, and Steven approaches both with the same standard. The technical side means understanding ERVs, dehumidification, fresh-air mechanics, and how the systems interact across multiple floors and zones. Steven services geothermal too. He doesn't advertise geothermal installation because he isn't set up to handle that scope, but he'll gladly take a service call on one. The relationships at this end of the market are worth it.

The presentation side matters just as much. Shoe covers come on. The truck stays clean and doesn't leak oil onto a driveway that probably cost more than Steven's house. Conversations stay calm and professional, on the phone and at the door. Steven knows these homeowners aren't picking ECS because it's the cheapest option. They're picking ECS because the work is correct and the person doing it represents the home well.

The first maintenance agreement ECS ever sold is in this market. A 10-system home in Sunset with a wine cellar, signed up on a $3,000 plan, which has grown into roughly $20,000 of work since.

## What ECS won't do or claim

ECS doesn't advertise geothermal installation. Service on geothermal is welcome. New systems in that category aren't ECS's scope, and the website needs to reflect that. No name-dropping famous customers. No implying ECS is a "luxury" company in a way that puts off the everyday homeowner who is just as welcome on the customer list.
