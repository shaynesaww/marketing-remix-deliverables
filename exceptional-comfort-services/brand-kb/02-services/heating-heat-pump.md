## What ECS does

ECS services, repairs, and maintains heat pump systems across the Upstate. Heat pumps are one of the most common setups in this part of South Carolina, which means Steven runs into them constantly. The work covers seasonal maintenance, reversing valve issues, defrost problems, zone board failures, refrigerant charge issues, and the full range of repairs a heat pump tends to need over its life.

## Who it's for

Homeowners running a heat pump as their primary system. People who want their unit serviced by somebody who has actually worked on heat pumps for years, not somebody who learned the basics last month and is reading the rest off a tablet in the driveway.

## How Steven approaches it

Heat pumps look simple on the outside and get complicated quickly underneath. Zone boards, reversing valves, defrost cycles, and the interplay between the air handler and the outdoor unit all need to be understood as one system. Steven has the hands-on experience to actually do that.

The story that captures it is the sewer-line crawl space call from his Carolina Heating days. A woman in a rental had no AC in summer. Most techs would have used the busted sewer line under the house as a reason to leave. Steven went in anyway, traced the problem to a failed zone board, and changed it the same day. Knowing the components and being willing to actually go look at them is the difference between a real diagnosis and a guess.

## What ECS won't do or claim

No selling a new system on a heat pump that can be repaired. No vague diagnoses that conveniently end with a replacement quote. If the unit truly is at the end of its life, Steven will walk the homeowner through why.
