# Exceptional Comfort Services — Post-Launch Roadmap

**The first 90 days after your new website goes live.**

This isn't a sales pitch. It's a map. So you know what to expect, what's normal, and where the natural decision points are.

---

## Week 1–2 → Build Sprint

**What we're doing:**
- Discovery, keyword research, competitor analysis
- Rewriting every page of your existing site
- Building 11 new pages (5 service pages, 5 location pages, Lake Keowee positioning page)
- Setting up lead capture forms, the Conversation AI chat agent, Google Business Profile integration
- A2P 10DLC registration kicked off in parallel

**What we need from you:**
- The intake document filled in
- Logo and brand asset folder
- Admin access to GBP, Housecall Pro (for domain), Google Analytics
- Quick turnarounds on draft approvals

**What it feels like:** Lots of back-and-forth. You'll see drafts mid-week and end-of-week. Approve, edit, push back — whatever you need.

---

## Week 3 → Launch Week

**What we're doing:**
- Domain transfer from Housecall Pro to a standard registrar
- DNS cutover, SSL setup, email forwarding
- Final QA: every link, every form, every page on mobile
- Recorded training session for you and your team — walks through the new site, the GoHighLevel CRM, how leads flow, how to use the GBP review automation
- Site goes live

**What you get:** A Project Summary document with everything — page assets, copy, SEO metadata, all logins, tracking setup, lead flow diagram, recommended follow-up process for your team.

---

## Weeks 3–4 → Indexing Window

**What's happening behind the scenes:**
- Google finds the new pages, starts crawling them
- Ranking signals begin
- Conversation AI agent is live and capturing chat leads
- Review request automation starts collecting reviews after every job
- Lead notifications firing to your phone and inbox

**What's normal:** No fireworks yet. Most pages start in positions 30+ and climb as Google figures out what they're about.
**What's not normal:** No leads at all after 2 weeks. If that happens, we look at form behavior, chat engagement, and traffic sources.

---

## Month 2 → First Performance Report

**You get your first monthly performance report, covering:**
- Keyword rankings and movement
- Total website traffic and source breakdown
- Lead count (forms + chat conversations + phone calls if tracked)
- Conversation AI chat activity
- GBP review activity

**What we're watching for:**
- Early ranking movement in low-competition keywords (Greer, Seneca, Easley)
- First leads through the new forms
- Review velocity from the automation

---

## Month 3 → Natural Decision Point

This is the month where the data tells a real story. Three months is the standard timeline for Google to make sense of a new site. By this point you'll see:

- Where you're ranking well and where you're not
- Which pages are pulling traffic and converting
- How the chat agent is performing
- How many net new reviews you've earned

**This is the conversation we'll have:** "Foundation's working. Here's where to invest next."

---

## The Four Natural Next Investments — In Priority Order

These are what I typically recommend after the foundation site is live and producing data. **No decisions today. This is just the map.**

### #1 — Google Business Profile Optimization
**Why first:** The competitor report showed the big Greenville brands win the map pack mostly on reputation, not on website. A GBP optimization push closes that gap with mostly one-time work — service category cleanup, post automation, photo additions, Q&A seeding.
**Effort:** Low. Mostly one-time.

### #2 — Review Generation Campaign
**Why second:** Reviews are the biggest single ranking lever for local home services. We already have the GBP review automation hooked up at launch. A focused campaign adds SMS follow-up, post-job triggers, and incentive structures for your techs.
**Effort:** Light ongoing. Compounds fast.

### #3 — Ongoing Content / New Pages Monthly
**Why third:** The 11 pages we build at launch get you in the door. Sustained ranking growth needs 2–4 new pages each month — long-tail service+location combos (think "heat pump install in Six Mile") and seasonal topics ("when to replace a 15-year-old AC in the Upstate").
**Effort:** Monthly. Builds momentum over 6–12 months.

### #4 — Paid Layer
**Why last:** Once organic foundation is healthy and converting, paid ads amplify it. Google Local Service Ads (the green-checkmark ads at the top) or paid search, or Facebook lead-gen for specific service campaigns.
**Effort:** Variable. Requires budget allocation.
