# Onboarding Call — Exceptional Comfort Services

**Client:** Steven Tibball, Exceptional Comfort Services
**Date:** 2026-05-18
**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Recorded:** Yes (with permission) — recording becomes raw material for site content

---

## Purpose of This Call

This call is **not** an operational data-gathering session. Operational items (EIN, photos, logins, service area, A2P info, etc.) live in a separate intake document Steven completes on his own time after the call.

This call exists for one reason: **to get Steven talking, on the record, about who he is, who his family is, and what he wants ECS to mean to people in the Upstate.** The recording becomes the source material for the About page, the owner bio, the team section, every "why us" paragraph on every service page, and the Lake Keowee content-positioning page.

**Time budget:** 45 of the 60 minutes are open-ended story-extraction. The remaining 15 are framing, post-launch roadmap, and send-off.

---

## 0:00–0:05 — Welcome & Frame the Call (5 min)

**Script:**

> "Hey Steven, thanks for blocking out the hour. Quick framing before we dive in.
>
> Two reasons I'm recording this call — and I want to confirm you're good with that. First, so I don't miss anything. Second, and honestly the bigger one — what you tell me today is what goes on your website. Your story, in your words, becomes the copy. So this call is mostly me asking questions and getting out of your way.
>
> The other thing — all the operational stuff like EIN, photos, logins, service area details, the certifications and warranty stuff — I'm sending you a document right after this call. You fill that in at your own pace. We're not going to waste this hour on data you can type into a form. We're going to spend it on you and your business, because that's the part that nobody else can give me."

**Confirm:** OK to record? Any hard stop before the hour?

## 0:05–0:25 — Steven, The Person (20 min) — LARGEST BLOCK

**Goal:** Source material for the About page, owner bio, "Meet Steven" section, and every "why us" paragraph on every service page.

**Note to Shayne:** Don't rush. Silence is fine. The longer his answers, the more usable the recording. If he gives a short answer, ask "tell me more about that" or "what was that like." Resist the urge to fill space.

**Questions:**

1. Where did you grow up? Are you from the Upstate originally, or did you find your way here?
2. What was your first real job? How did you end up in HVAC specifically?
3. Who taught you the trade? Was there a mentor, or a moment where it clicked for you?
4. How long have you been doing this — total years in the trade, not just at ECS?
5. What certifications did you earn the hard way?
   *(NATE, EPA Universal, state license — let him brag a little. We capture the formal list in the intake doc; here we want the story behind earning them.)*
6. Walk me through the moment you decided "I'm starting my own company." What was happening in your life right then?
7. What's the hardest job you've ever been on — technically or emotionally?
8. What's the job you're proudest of?
9. What do you want your name to mean to a homeowner in Pickens five years from now?

## 0:25–0:40 — The Family Business & The Team (15 min)

**Goal:** Family-business positioning. Steven mentioned on the proposal call he wants this to grow into a family business. That's a major thrust of the brand and the website narrative.

**Questions:**

1. Tell me about your family. Wife's name? Kids? How old?
2. Is your wife involved in the business today? What does she do?
   *(If not, skip the next question.)*
3. Is she the kind of person whose photo and name should be on the site as part of the team, or does she prefer to stay behind the scenes?
4. The kids — are they involved at all? Answering phones, riding along, anything? Do you see them in the business someday?
5. When you say "I want this to be a family business" — paint that picture for me. What does ECS look like in 10 years if everything goes right?
6. Who's on the team today? Is it mostly you running point right now, or do you have anyone alongside you? Walk me through whoever's in the picture.

---

**The next block is the heart of the recording.** Steven's largely a one-man show right now, which means his *own* stories about the work are what the site's credibility is built on. Slow these down. Use silence. Follow the thread when something hits.

7. Tell me about a job that, when you look back on it, you think: "*That's* exactly the kind of work I want my name attached to." Walk me through what happened.

8. Has there ever been a job where you went way beyond what you were paid to do — because it was just the right thing to do? What was that?

9. Has the Lord ever put a customer in front of you where the call turned into something more than HVAC — somebody who needed something beyond just the unit being fixed?
   *(Soft faith-language question — if it doesn't land, just ask: "A job that felt like more than a service call — somebody who needed something beyond getting the unit fixed.")*

10. A moment where you could've cut a corner — saved yourself time, saved yourself money, nobody would've known — and you didn't. Why not?

11. Is there a customer who's stayed with you over the years? Somebody whose house you keep coming back to, whose neighbors or family you've ended up serving too? Paint that picture for me.

12. Tell me about a homeowner who'd been burned before — somebody who'd been overcharged or sold something they didn't need by another company — and what it was like serving them after.

13. Have you ever had to make a hard call on a job — tell a customer something they didn't want to hear, or walk away from a job because it wasn't right? What happened?

14. *(If he has techs or anyone alongside him)* Is there a story about somebody on the team that captures the kind of team you're building?

15. When you show up at a homeowner's door — the first 60 seconds, before you've said much — what do you want them to immediately notice about you?

16. As ECS grows and you bring techs on, what's the culture you want them to walk into? What's the kind of person you'd hire — and the kind you'd never hire?

## 0:40–0:50 — Brand Feeling, Customers & The Right Fit (10 min)

**Goal:** Extract the tone, voice, and differentiation. Goes on the homepage, every service page, and the Lake Keowee positioning page.

**Questions:**

1. When a customer hangs up the phone after talking with ECS, how do you want them to feel?
2. Describe the kind of customer you love serving. Who is that person? What's their house like? What does the conversation usually sound like?
3. Tell me about a "Steven moment" — something you've done for a customer that the big franchised guys would never do. A small thing or a big thing, doesn't matter.
4. What gets you genuinely angry about how HVAC gets done in this industry?
   *(This is gold for the "why us" copy.)*
5. A homeowner in Greenville or Easley is sitting on hold with one of the big brands right now. What would you tell them, in one sentence, about why they should hang up and call you instead?
6. The lake-home folks at Keowee and Jocassee — what's different about serving those customers? Any stories there?
7. Anything else you want a customer to know about you that they'd never know from a Google search?

## 0:50–0:58 — Roadmap: What Happens After Launch (8 min)

**Goal:** Walk Steven through the post-launch flow so he knows what to expect. Pre-frame the natural next-step investments without selling.

**Script:**

> "Last thing before we wrap. I want to walk you through what happens after we hit launch, so you're not wondering what month two looks like."

Walk through the roadmap document (separate file):

- **Week 1–2 — Build Sprint:** Discovery, content rewriting, 11 new pages, lead forms, Conversation AI agent, GBP integration. Heavy back-and-forth as drafts come in for approval.
- **Week 3 — Launch Week:** Domain cutover, final QA, recorded training session, site goes live.
- **Weeks 3–4 — Indexing Window:** Google finds the pages. Ranking signals start. Conversation AI live. Reviews automation collecting.
- **Month 2 — First Performance Report:** Early ranking movement, first leads through new forms.
- **Month 3 — Natural Decision Point:** Performance data tells a real story. Decision time on where to invest next.
- **Month 3+ — Ongoing Rhythm:** Monthly reporting, ongoing site updates, expansion conversations.

Then pre-frame the natural next investments:

> "Once the foundation is live, here's what I'd typically recommend in priority order. Don't decide anything today. I want you to see it now so when we have the conversation in two months, it's not new information."

1. **GBP optimization + weekly post automation** — the competitor report shows the big guys win the map pack on reputation alone. Closing that gap is mostly one-time work.
2. **Review generation campaign** — automated review requests post-job. Biggest single ranking lever for local HVAC.
3. **Ongoing content / new pages each month** — the 11 pages get you in the door. Sustained growth needs 2–4 new pages monthly covering long-tail service+location combos.
4. **Paid layer** — Google Local Service Ads or Facebook lead-gen, once the organic foundation is healthy.

> "After we have the first month of data, we'll have a real conversation about which of those makes sense first."
